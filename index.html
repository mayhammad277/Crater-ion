Safe, Autonomous Precision Landing via Multi-Agent AI.

The Problem

Traditional autonomous landing systems struggle to balance complex, competing priorities. Managing fuel efficiency, scanning for unpredictable surface hazards, and executing split-second thrust adjustments simultaneously often leads to overwhelmed monolithic systems, resulting in unsafe mission failures or wasted resources.

The crater-ion Solution

We approach precision landing through a cutting-edge Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) architecture. Instead of relying on a single, easily overwhelmed AI, crater-ion deploys a coordinated team of four specialized agents working toward a shared mission goal (precision landing under 50m with optimal fuel efficiency):

  • Agent 1 (Perception): Maps craters with high confidence while aggressively minimizing false detections.
  • Agent 2 (Hazard Scoring): Ranks landing hazards in real-time with minimal update latency.
  • Agent 3 (Control/Thrust): Manages smooth, efficient trajectories and safe lunar far and near approach as well as touchdown velocities.
  • Agent 4 (Sensor Management): Optimizes scanning to rapidly reduce uncertainty without wasting energy on redundant sensing.

The Safety Advantage

Our system separates how the AI learns from how it executes. During training, a Centralized Critic evaluates all agent actions jointly to ensure perfect team coordination. At execution time, the agents operate independently but are safeguarded by a critical failsafe.

Intrinsic Safety by Design: We utilize a hard safety layer known as a Control Barrier Function (CBF). Our control agent (Agent 3) is penalized for how often the CBF has to intervene—not just for crashing. This means our AI voluntarily learns to stay strictly within safe operational bounds, making the failsafe rarely needed.
